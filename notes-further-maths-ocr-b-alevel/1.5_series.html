1.5: Series

1. Summation Notation

\(\displaystyle\sum_{r=a}^{b}f(r)=f(a)+f(a+1)+\cdots+f(b)\). Summation is linear:

\(\displaystyle\sum[\lambda f(r)+\mu g(r)]=\lambda\sum f(r)+\mu\sum g(r)\)

2. Standard Sums

\(\displaystyle\sum_{r=1}^{n}1=n,\quad \sum_{r=1}^{n}r=\tfrac{n(n+1)}{2}\)
\(\displaystyle\sum_{r=1}^{n}r^2=\tfrac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6},\quad \sum_{r=1}^{n}r^3=\left[\tfrac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]^2\)
Find \(\sum_{r=1}^{n}(2r^2-3r+1)\).
\(=\tfrac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{3}-\tfrac{3n(n+1)}{2}+n=\tfrac{n(4n^2-3n-1)}{6}\) (simplify) [factor and check]

Shifting Limits

\(\displaystyle\sum_{r=a}^{b}f(r)=\sum_{r=1}^{b}f(r)-\sum_{r=1}^{a-1}f(r)\)

3. Method of Differences

If \(f(r)=g(r+1)-g(r)\), then \(\sum_{r=1}^{n}f(r)=g(n+1)-g(1)\) (telescoping).
Show \(\dfrac{1}{r(r+1)}=\dfrac{1}{r}-\dfrac{1}{r+1}\). Hence \(\sum_{r=1}^{n}\dfrac{1}{r(r+1)}\).
Partial fractions give the identity. Telescoping: \(1-\tfrac{1}{n+1}=\tfrac{n}{n+1}\).

Using an Identity Provided

Exam questions often give a useful identity like \(r(r+1)(r+2)-(r-1)r(r+1)=3r(r+1)\), and ask you to use it to find \(\sum r(r+1)\).

4. Maclaurin Series

\(f(x)=\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\dfrac{f^{(n)}(0)}{n!}x^n\).

Building New Series

  • Substitute: \(e^{-2x}\) from \(e^x\) by replacing \(x\to-2x\).
  • Multiply: truncate and multiply products (e.g. \(e^x\cos x\)).
  • Differentiate / integrate term-by-term within the radius.

5. Taylor Series

Expansion of \(f\) about \(x=a\):

\(f(x)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\dfrac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!}(x-a)^n\)

6. Applications

Approximations

Small-\(x\): \(\sin x\approx x\); \(\cos x\approx1-\tfrac{x^2}{2}\); \(e^x\approx1+x\).

Limits

\(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to0}\dfrac{1-\cos x}{x^2}=\dfrac{1}{2}\), by substituting series.
Exam Tip: Note convergence explicitly (especially for \(\ln\), \((1+x)^n\) with non-integer \(n\)). When building products, keep only the terms up to the requested order.
