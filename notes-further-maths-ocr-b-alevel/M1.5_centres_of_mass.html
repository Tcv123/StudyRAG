M1.5: Centres of Mass

1. Definition

For a system of particles with masses \(m_i\) at positions \(\mathbf{r}_i\), the centre of mass is \[\bar{\mathbf{r}}=\dfrac{\sum_i m_i\mathbf{r}_i}{\sum_i m_i}.\] In uniform gravity, CoM coincides with the centre of gravity.

2. Uniform Laminae (2D)

For a region under \(y=f(x)\) between \(x=a, x=b\):

\(\bar{x}=\dfrac{\int_a^b xf(x)\,dx}{\int_a^b f(x)\,dx},\quad \bar{y}=\dfrac{\tfrac{1}{2}\int_a^b[f(x)]^2\,dx}{\int_a^b f(x)\,dx}\)

3. Solids of Revolution

About \(x\)-axis, \(y=f(x)\), \(a\le x\le b\):

\(\bar{x}=\dfrac{\int x[f(x)]^2\,dx}{\int [f(x)]^2\,dx}\)

5. Equilibrium and Toppling

A body on a horizontal surface is in equilibrium if the vertical through CoM falls within the base of support.
