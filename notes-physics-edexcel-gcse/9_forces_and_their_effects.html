Edexcel GCSE (9-1) Physics (1PH0) — Paper 2

9.1 Vectors and Resultant Forces

Forces are vectors — they have magnitude and direction. When adding, take direction into account.

5 N right 4 N 3 N Resultant = √(3²+4²) = 5 N

9.2 Moments, Levers and Gears

Moment of a force = F × perpendicular distance from the pivot. Unit: newton-metre (N·m).
Principle of moments: sum of clockwise = sum of anticlockwise (for equilibrium)

Gears

  • Small gear driving a large gear: speed decreases, torque increases.
  • Used in bikes (low gear = high torque for hills) and in cars (gearboxes).

9.3 Centre of Mass

The single point where we consider an object\'s weight to act. Found experimentally for flat shapes using a plumb line (hang from two points; centre of mass is at the intersection of the two drawn lines).

9.4 Hooke\'s Law

F = k × x   (k = spring constant, N/m)

Valid up to the limit of proportionality. Beyond the elastic limit, the spring is permanently deformed (inelastic deformation).

Elastic PE = ½ × k × x²

Key Points Summary

  • Forces are vectors; combine by considering direction.
  • Moment = F × perpendicular d; balanced when CW = ACW moments.
  • Gears trade speed for torque — used in cars and bikes.
  • Hooke\'s law: F = kx up to limit of proportionality; elastic PE = ½kx².
