Topic 13 — Electromagnetic Induction

Edexcel GCSE (9-1) Physics (1PH0) — Paper 2

13.1 Inducing an EMF

Moving a conductor through a magnetic field, or changing the magnetic field through a coil, induces an EMF (potential difference). If part of a circuit, an induced current will flow.

Ways to increase induced EMF:

  • Move the magnet (or wire) faster;
  • Use a stronger magnet;
  • Increase the number of turns on the coil;
  • Use a soft iron core.

13.2 Generators (Alternators)

A coil rotates in a magnetic field; the changing flux induces an alternating EMF. A simple AC generator uses slip rings and brushes; the output is AC.

13.3 Transformers

A transformer changes the voltage of an alternating supply. It has a primary coil and a secondary coil, both wound on a soft iron core. Alternating current in the primary creates a changing magnetic flux, inducing an AC in the secondary.
V_s / V_p = N_s / N_p V_p I_p = V_s I_s   (ideal transformer — power in = power out)

Step-up vs Step-down

  • Step-up: more turns on secondary → higher V_s, lower I_s.
  • Step-down: fewer turns on secondary → lower V_s, higher I_s.
Primary 230 V, N_p = 100, N_s = 1000.
V_s = 230 × 1000/100 = 2300 V.
If I_p = 4 A, I_s = 230 × 4 / 2300 = 0.40 A.

13.4 National Grid and Transmission Losses

Transmission lines dissipate power as I²R. Doubling the voltage halves the current for the same power → quarters the heat loss. Hence very high voltages (up to 400 kV) are used, then stepped down for safe use.

Transformers need changing current — they don\'t work with steady DC because a constant flux induces no EMF.

13.5 Everyday Uses

  • Microphones: sound vibrates a diaphragm/coil in a field → induced EMF.
  • Dynamos and alternators on bicycles / in cars.
  • Induction cooking, wireless phone charging, electric toothbrushes.

Key Points Summary

  • Induced EMF requires a change in flux through a coil.
  • Increase EMF by faster movement, stronger magnet, more turns, iron core.
  • Generators produce AC using slip rings; DC dynamos use a commutator.
  • Transformers: V_s/V_p = N_s/N_p; power conserved (ideal).
  • National Grid: step up for transmission (low I → low I²R loss), step down near towns.
