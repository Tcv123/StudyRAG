Topic 15 — Forces and Matter

Edexcel GCSE (9-1) Physics (1PH0) — Paper 2

15.1 Elastic Behaviour and Hooke\'s Law

F = k × x

Valid only up to the limit of proportionality. Up to the elastic limit, deformation is elastic (object returns to original shape); beyond it, deformation is inelastic/plastic (permanent).

Elastic PE = ½ k x²
Spring of k = 250 N/m stretched by 0.10 m.
F = 250 × 0.10 = 25 N; E = ½ × 250 × 0.01 = 1.25 J.

15.2 Pressure in Fluids

p = F / A   (pascal = N/m²) p = ρ × g × h   (pressure at depth h in a fluid)
Water density 1 000 kg/m³, g = 10. Pressure at 15 m depth = 1 000 × 10 × 15 = 150 000 Pa (= 1.5 × 10⁵ Pa).

15.3 Upthrust and Floating

Upthrust arises because pressure on the bottom of a submerged object is greater than on the top. An object floats when upthrust = weight.

A steel ship floats because the hull shape displaces enough water that the average density of the ship is less than water.

15.4 Atmospheric Pressure

Air has weight. Atmospheric pressure at sea level ≈ 10⁵ Pa. Pressure decreases with altitude because there is less air above.

15.5 Hydraulic Systems

Pressure is transmitted equally in an incompressible fluid. A small force on a small piston produces a larger force on a bigger piston (same pressure × greater area). Used in car brakes and lifts.

Key Points Summary

  • Hooke\'s law: F = kx up to limit of proportionality; elastic PE = ½kx².
  • Pressure p = F/A (Pa = N/m²); in fluids p = ρgh.
  • Upthrust arises from pressure difference; floats when upthrust = weight.
  • Atmospheric pressure ≈ 10⁵ Pa, decreases with altitude.
  • Hydraulics multiply forces using equal pressure across areas.
