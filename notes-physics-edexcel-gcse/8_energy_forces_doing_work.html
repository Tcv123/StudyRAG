Topic 8 — Energy: Forces Doing Work

Edexcel GCSE (9-1) Physics (1PH0) — Paper 2

8.1 Work Done

Work done by a force = force × distance moved in the direction of the force. It equals the energy transferred.
W = F × d   (joule, J; 1 J = 1 N·m)
A 25 N force pushes a box 3 m horizontally in the direction of the force.
W = 25 × 3 = 75 J. This 75 J is transferred to kinetic energy (and heat via friction).
If the force is perpendicular to the motion, it does no work (e.g. gravity on a horizontal slide).

8.2 Power

P = E ÷ t = W ÷ t

Unit: watt (W) = 1 J/s. Also P = F × v (force × velocity) for a constant speed.

A motor lifts a 20 kg load 4 m in 5 s (g = 10).
Useful work = mgh = 800 J. Useful power = 800/5 = 160 W.

8.3 Efficiency of Machines

η = useful energy out ÷ total energy in (× 100%)

In real machines, friction and resistance dissipate energy as heat and sound. Reduce by lubrication, streamlining, and good bearing design.

8.4 Energy and Machines

  • A lever applies a smaller effort force over a larger distance to move a larger load over a smaller distance.
  • A pulley can change the direction of a force and reduce the effort needed.
  • A gear changes the trade-off between torque and rotational speed.

Key Points Summary

  • Work done = F × d (in direction of F); unit joule.
  • Power = W/t; unit watt.
  • Efficiency is always < 100% due to friction/heat/sound.
  • Machines trade force for distance (or vice versa) but can\'t beat energy conservation.
