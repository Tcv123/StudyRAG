Topic 5 — Light and the EM Spectrum

Edexcel GCSE (9-1) Physics (1PH0) — Paper 1

5.1 The EM Spectrum

Electromagnetic (EM) waves are transverse and all travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s in a vacuum. They differ in frequency and wavelength.

5.3 Reflection and Refraction

At a boundary, light can reflect (angle of incidence = angle of reflection) or refract.

5.4 Lenses

  • Convex (converging) lenses focus parallel rays to a focal point — used in cameras, the eye, telescopes.
  • Concave (diverging) lenses spread rays outwards; used for short-sight correction.

5.5 Colour and Filters

  • A red object looks red because it reflects red light and absorbs others.
  • A blue filter only transmits blue; absorbs other colours.

5.6 Total Internal Reflection

When light inside a dense medium strikes a boundary at an angle greater than the critical angle, all light reflects back — used in optical fibres and endoscopes.

Key Points Summary

  • All EM waves travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s in vacuum and are transverse.
  • Know uses/hazards in order: radio, micro, IR, visible, UV, X, γ.
  • Higher frequency = more energy; UV, X and γ are ionising.
  • Refraction = change in speed → change in direction.
  • Total internal reflection above critical angle → optical fibres.
