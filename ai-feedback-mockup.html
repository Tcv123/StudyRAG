Q. Assess the relative importance of physical and human factors in increasing flood risk.
Don't like this question? Generate another · Browse all 12-mark Hazards questions
2. Your answer
347 words · 2 min 14 sec

Flood risk is increased by both physical and human factors. Physical factors include heavy rainfall, impermeable rock and steep relief. For example, in Cumbria 2015, Storm Desmond brought 341mm of rain in 24 hours, which led to widespread flooding in Carlisle and Keswick. Saturated ground meant that infiltration was minimal, causing rapid surface runoff.

Human factors are also important. Urbanisation creates impermeable surfaces and storm drains that speed up runoff and shorten lag times. Deforestation reduces interception and evapotranspiration. Channel modification can transfer flood risk downstream.

In conclusion, both physical and human factors are important in increasing flood risk and they often work together.

✦ Model paragraph (top band)

While extreme rainfall is the proximate trigger of flooding, human land-use change determines the scale of the loss. In Cumbria 2015, 341mm of rain fell in 24 hours — but the £500 million damage in Carlisle reflected decades of floodplain development and channel constriction. By contrast, a comparable rainfall event in a forested catchment such as the upper Eden would have produced a far smaller flood. On balance, therefore, human factors are the more decisive influence on flood risk, since they translate hazard into disaster.
