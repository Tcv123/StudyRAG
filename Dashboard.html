◎ Diagnostics

Test your knowledge on any topic below. Each diagnostic gives you a mix of questions and updates your RAG colour based on how you score — green means confident, amber means almost there, and red means it needs more revision.

👋 Welcome to RAG Learning
Start with a diagnostic — it'll pinpoint your weak topics so your revision targets what actually matters.
1
Take a diagnostic (highly recommended) — open the Diagnostic tab and work through the topics for each subject. It gives you a personalised red/amber/green map of what to prioritise. Your notes and practice questions are unlocked from day one, but skipping the diagnostic means revising blind.
2
Check your Breakdown page to see which topics came back red or amber — those are the ones that need the most work.
3
Use Notes and Practice Questions to drill your weak topics — they're available anytime, but the diagnostic tells you where to start.
4
Re-do diagnostics to update your RAG colours as you improve.
The goal is to get every topic green and feel confident heading into your exams. 💪
