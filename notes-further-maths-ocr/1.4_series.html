OCR A-Level Further Mathematics (H245) — Core Pure Section 1.4

1.4: Series

1. Summation Notation

\(\sum_{r=a}^{b} f(r) = f(a) + f(a+1) + \cdots + f(b)\)  — \(r\) is the index, \(a\) and \(b\) the lower and upper limits.

\(\sum_{r=1}^{n} [\lambda f(r) + \mu g(r)] = \lambda \sum_{r=1}^{n} f(r) + \mu \sum_{r=1}^{n} g(r)\)

Summation is linear — constants factor out, sums split over addition.

2. Standard Sums

You are expected to know and use:

\(\displaystyle \sum_{r=1}^{n} 1 = n\)
\(\displaystyle \sum_{r=1}^{n} r = \tfrac{1}{2}n(n+1)\)
\(\displaystyle \sum_{r=1}^{n} r^2 = \tfrac{1}{6}n(n+1)(2n+1)\)
\(\displaystyle \sum_{r=1}^{n} r^3 = \tfrac{1}{4}n^2(n+1)^2 = \left[\tfrac{1}{2}n(n+1)\right]^2\)
OCR Exam Tip: These four formulae are given in the formula booklet but it saves time to have them memorised. Note the neat identity \(\sum r^3 = (\sum r)^2\).

Shifting Limits

Sum from \(a\) to \(b\) via subtraction:

\(\displaystyle \sum_{r=a}^{b} f(r) = \sum_{r=1}^{b} f(r) - \sum_{r=1}^{a-1} f(r)\)
Find \(\sum_{r=1}^{n}(3r^2 - 2r + 5)\) as a polynomial in \(n\).
\(= 3\cdot\tfrac{1}{6}n(n+1)(2n+1) - 2\cdot\tfrac{1}{2}n(n+1) + 5n\)
\(= \tfrac{1}{2}n(n+1)(2n+1) - n(n+1) + 5n\)
\(= \tfrac{1}{2}n[(n+1)(2n+1) - 2(n+1) + 10]\)
\(= \tfrac{1}{2}n[(n+1)(2n-1) + 10] = \tfrac{1}{2}n(2n^2 + n - 1 + 10) = \tfrac{1}{2}n(2n^2 + n + 9)\).

3. Method of Differences

If \(f(r) = u_{r+1} - u_r\) (a "telescoping" difference), then

\(\displaystyle \sum_{r=1}^{n} f(r) = u_{n+1} - u_1.\)

Most terms cancel. The trick is to recognise or construct an expression as a difference of consecutive terms — often using partial fractions.

Show that \(\dfrac{1}{r(r+1)} = \dfrac{1}{r} - \dfrac{1}{r+1}\), and hence find \(\sum_{r=1}^{n} \dfrac{1}{r(r+1)}\).
The identity follows from partial fractions.
\(\displaystyle \sum_{r=1}^{n}\left(\tfrac{1}{r} - \tfrac{1}{r+1}\right) = \left(1 - \tfrac{1}{2}\right) + \left(\tfrac{1}{2} - \tfrac{1}{3}\right) + \cdots + \left(\tfrac{1}{n} - \tfrac{1}{n+1}\right) = 1 - \tfrac{1}{n+1} = \dfrac{n}{n+1}.\)
Use method of differences to find \(\sum_{r=1}^{n} \dfrac{1}{(r+1)(r+2)}\).
\(\dfrac{1}{(r+1)(r+2)} = \dfrac{1}{r+1} - \dfrac{1}{r+2}\). Telescoping gives \(\dfrac{1}{2} - \dfrac{1}{n+2} = \dfrac{n}{2(n+2)}\).
OCR Exam Tip: When a question gives you a surprising identity to "show", the identity is almost always a telescoping difference — use it in the next part to sum the series.

4. Maclaurin Series

The Maclaurin series of \(f\) is \[f(x) = f(0) + f'(0)\,x + \dfrac{f''(0)}{2!}x^2 + \dfrac{f'''(0)}{3!}x^3 + \cdots = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{f^{(n)}(0)}{n!} x^n\] valid (and equal to \(f\)) within some interval of convergence.

Standard Maclaurin Series (with domain of validity)

FunctionExpansionValid for
\(e^x\)\(1 + x + \tfrac{x^2}{2!} + \tfrac{x^3}{3!} + \cdots\)All \(x\)
\(\sin x\)\(x - \tfrac{x^3}{3!} + \tfrac{x^5}{5!} - \cdots\)All \(x\)
\(\cos x\)\(1 - \tfrac{x^2}{2!} + \tfrac{x^4}{4!} - \cdots\)All \(x\)
\(\ln(1+x)\)\(x - \tfrac{x^2}{2} + \tfrac{x^3}{3} - \cdots\)\(-1 < x \le 1\)
\((1+x)^k\)\(1 + kx + \tfrac{k(k-1)}{2!}x^2 + \cdots\)\(|x| < 1\) (if \(k \notin \mathbb{Z}^+\))

Building New Series

  • Substitution: to get the series for \(e^{-2x}\), substitute \(-2x\) into the \(e^x\) series.
  • Multiplication: multiply two series term by term to the required order.
  • Differentiation / integration: differentiate or integrate term by term within the interval of convergence.
Find the Maclaurin series of \(e^x \cos x\) up to and including the term in \(x^4\).
\(e^x = 1 + x + \tfrac{x^2}{2} + \tfrac{x^3}{6} + \tfrac{x^4}{24} + \cdots\)
\(\cos x = 1 - \tfrac{x^2}{2} + \tfrac{x^4}{24} - \cdots\)
Product up to \(x^4\):
\(1(1) + 1(x) + \left(\tfrac{x^2}{2} - \tfrac{x^2}{2}\right) + \left(\tfrac{x^3}{6} - \tfrac{x^3}{2}\right) + \left(\tfrac{x^4}{24} - \tfrac{x^4}{4} + \tfrac{x^4}{24}\right)\)
\(= 1 + x + 0 - \tfrac{x^3}{3} - \tfrac{x^4}{6}\).

5. Taylor Series (General Point)

The Taylor expansion of \(f\) about \(x = a\) is

\(f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!} (x - a)^n.\)

Use this for approximating \(f\) near \(x = a\) when \(a \ne 0\).

6. Applications of Series

Approximations

Truncate a Maclaurin/Taylor series at a sensible order. For small \(x\), \(\sin x \approx x\), \(\cos x \approx 1 - \tfrac{x^2}{2}\), \(e^x \approx 1 + x\).

Limits

Substitute series expansions to evaluate limits of \(0/0\) type:

\(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to 0} \dfrac{1 - \cos x}{x^2}\).
\(\cos x = 1 - \tfrac{x^2}{2} + \tfrac{x^4}{24} - \cdots\), so \(1 - \cos x = \tfrac{x^2}{2} - \tfrac{x^4}{24} + \cdots\).
Divide by \(x^2\): \(\tfrac{1}{2} - \tfrac{x^2}{24} + \cdots \to \tfrac{1}{2}\).

7. Exam Style Strategy

  • Recognise standard sums quickly and factorise aggressively to simplify.
  • Partial fractions are the standard stepping stone to the method of differences.
  • When building a Maclaurin series by product, carry only the terms you need and discard higher powers.
  • Always state (or check) the radius of convergence for non-polynomial series.
