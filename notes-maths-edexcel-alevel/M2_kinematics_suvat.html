M2: Constant Acceleration — Kinematics (SUVAT)

1. SUVAT Variables

The five SUVAT variables describe motion under constant acceleration:
  • \(s\) — displacement (m) — change in position from start (vector: can be negative)
  • \(u\) — initial velocity (m s⁻¹) — velocity at \(t = 0\)
  • \(v\) — final velocity (m s⁻¹) — velocity at time \(t\)
  • \(a\) — acceleration (m s⁻²) — constant throughout
  • \(t\) — time (s) — duration of motion
Exam tip: SUVAT equations only apply when acceleration is constant. If acceleration varies, you must use calculus (differentiation/integration).

2. The SUVAT Equations

There are five SUVAT equations — each one omits one variable. Identify the unknown and the known quantities, then select the equation that does not involve the unwanted variable.

EquationOmits
\(v = u + at\)\(s\)
\(s = ut + \dfrac{1}{2}at^2\)\(v\)
\(s = vt - \dfrac{1}{2}at^2\)\(u\)
\(v^2 = u^2 + 2as\)\(t\)
\(s = \dfrac{1}{2}(u+v)t\)\(a\)
Example: A car accelerates from rest at 2 m s⁻² for 8 seconds. Find its final velocity and the distance travelled.

Known: \(u = 0\), \(a = 2\), \(t = 8\). Find \(v\) and \(s\).

\(v = u + at = 0 + 2 \times 8 = 16 \text{ m s}^{-1}\)
\(s = ut + \tfrac{1}{2}at^2 = 0 + \tfrac{1}{2} \times 2 \times 64 = 64 \text{ m}\)

Check with \(v^2 = u^2 + 2as\): \(16^2 = 0 + 2 \times 2 \times 64 = 256\). ✓

3. Vertical Motion Under Gravity

When an object moves vertically, the only force is gravity (ignoring air resistance), giving a constant downward acceleration of \(g = 9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}\).

Convention: take upward as positive. Then \(a = -9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}\) (downward).
If downward is taken as positive: \(a = +9.8 \text{ m s}^{-2}\) — be consistent throughout!

At the highest point, velocity = 0. The motion is symmetric: time up = time down (if returning to same level).

Example: A ball is thrown upward with speed 15 m s⁻¹. Find the maximum height reached and the time of flight before it returns to the same level.

Taking upward as positive: \(u = 15\), \(a = -9.8\), \(v = 0\) at max height.

Max height: \(v^2 = u^2 + 2as\): \(0 = 225 - 19.6s\) → \(s = \dfrac{225}{19.6} \approx 11.5 \text{ m}\)

Time to max height: \(v = u + at\): \(0 = 15 - 9.8t\) → \(t = \dfrac{15}{9.8} \approx 1.53 \text{ s}\)

Total flight time: By symmetry, \(T = 2 \times 1.53 \approx 3.06 \text{ s}\)

5. Multi-Stage Problems

Many problems involve two or more stages with different constant accelerations. Apply SUVAT separately to each stage. The final velocity of one stage is the initial velocity of the next.

Example: A train starts from rest and accelerates at 0.5 m s⁻² for 60 s. It then travels at constant velocity for 3 minutes before decelerating to rest at 1 m s⁻². Find the total distance travelled.

Stage 1 (acceleration): \(u=0\), \(a=0.5\), \(t=60\)
\(v = 0 + 0.5 \times 60 = 30 \text{ m s}^{-1}\)
\(s_1 = 0 + \tfrac{1}{2} \times 0.5 \times 3600 = 900 \text{ m}\)

Stage 2 (constant velocity): \(u = v = 30\), \(t = 180\)
\(s_2 = 30 \times 180 = 5400 \text{ m}\)

Stage 3 (deceleration): \(u=30\), \(v=0\), \(a=-1\)
\(v^2 = u^2 + 2as \Rightarrow 0 = 900 - 2s_3 \Rightarrow s_3 = 450 \text{ m}\)

Total: \(s = 900 + 5400 + 450 = 6750 \text{ m}\)

6. Connected Particles

When two particles are connected by a light inextensible string over a smooth light pulley:

  • Both particles have the same magnitude of acceleration.
  • The tension \(T\) is the same throughout the string.
  • Write separate Newton's Second Law equations for each particle, then solve simultaneously.
Example: Masses of 5 kg and 3 kg are connected by a light inextensible string over a smooth pulley. The system is released from rest. Find the acceleration and the tension.

For the 5 kg mass (heavier, moving down): \(5g - T = 5a\) → \(49 - T = 5a\)   (1)
For the 3 kg mass (moving up): \(T - 3g = 3a\) → \(T - 29.4 = 3a\)   (2)

Add (1) and (2): \(49 - 29.4 = 8a\) → \(a = \dfrac{19.6}{8} = 2.45 \text{ m s}^{-2}\)
From (2): \(T = 29.4 + 3 \times 2.45 = 29.4 + 7.35 = 36.75 \text{ N} \approx 36.8 \text{ N}\)
