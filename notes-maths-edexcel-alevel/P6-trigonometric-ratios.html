P6: Trigonometric Ratios

1. SOHCAHTOA in Right-Angled Triangles

For a right-angled triangle with angle \(\theta\): $$\sin\theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}, \quad \cos\theta = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}, \quad \tan\theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$$

Mnemonic: SOH CAH TOA

3. The Sine Rule

$$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$$

Use when: given two angles and one side (AAS/ASA), or two sides and a non-included angle (SSA — beware ambiguous case).

Example: In triangle ABC, \(A = 40°\), \(B = 75°\), \(a = 8\) cm. Find \(b\).

\(\dfrac{b}{\sin 75°} = \dfrac{8}{\sin 40°}\)
\(b = \dfrac{8 \sin 75°}{\sin 40°} = \dfrac{8 \times 0.9659}{0.6428} \approx 12.02\) cm

Ambiguous Case (SSA)

When given two sides \(a, b\) and angle \(A\) (not the included angle), there may be two possible triangles. Always check whether \(\sin B > 1\) (no solution), \(\sin B = 1\) (one solution), or \(\sin B < 1\) with \(B\) acute and obtuse versions both valid.

Exam Tip: When using the sine rule to find an angle, always consider whether the obtuse version of the angle is also valid. Check by seeing if the angles still sum to less than 180°.

4. The Cosine Rule

$$a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$$

Rearranged to find angle A:

$$\cos A = \frac{b^2 + c^2 - a^2}{2bc}$$

Use when: given three sides (SSS), or two sides and the included angle (SAS).

Example: In triangle PQR, \(p = 7\), \(q = 5\), \(R = 60°\). Find \(r\).

\(r^2 = 7^2 + 5^2 - 2(7)(5)\cos 60° = 49 + 25 - 70 \times \frac{1}{2} = 74 - 35 = 39\)
\(r = \sqrt{39} \approx 6.24\) cm

5. Area of a Triangle

$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}\,ab\sin C$$

Use when two sides and the included angle are known.

Example: Find the area of a triangle with sides 6 cm and 9 cm and included angle 50°.

Area \(= \frac{1}{2} \times 6 \times 9 \times \sin 50° = 27 \times 0.7660 \approx 20.7\) cm²

6. Unit Circle and CAST Diagram

The unit circle defines trig functions for all angles. For an angle \(\theta\) from the positive x-axis, the point on the unit circle is \((\cos\theta, \sin\theta)\).

CAST rule:
  • Q1 (0°–90°): All of sin, cos, tan positive
  • Q2 (90°–180°): Sin positive only
  • Q3 (180°–270°): Tan positive only
  • Q4 (270°–360°): Cos positive only

Related angles (using the CAST principle):

QuadrantRelated angleExample
Q2\(180° - \theta\)\(\sin 150° = \sin 30° = \frac{1}{2}\)
Q3\(180° + \theta\)\(\tan 210° = \tan 30° = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\)
Q4\(360° - \theta\)\(\cos 330° = \cos 30° = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)

Key symmetry identities:

$$\sin(180° - x) = \sin x \qquad \cos(180° - x) = -\cos x$$
$$\sin(-x) = -\sin x \qquad \cos(-x) = \cos x \qquad \tan(-x) = -\tan x$$
Exam Tip: When solving \(\sin\theta = k\) for \(0° \leq \theta \leq 360°\), find the principal value \(\theta_1 = \arcsin k\), then use CAST or the formula \(\theta_2 = 180° - \theta_1\) for the second solution. For \(\cos\theta = k\): second solution is \(\theta_2 = 360° - \theta_1\). For \(\tan\theta = k\): second solution is \(\theta_1 + 180°\).
