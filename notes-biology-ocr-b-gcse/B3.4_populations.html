B3.4 How are populations affected by conditions in an ecosystem?

OCR B GCSE Biology (J257) Twenty First Century Science — Topic B3: Living together — food and ecosystems

1. Abiotic and biotic factors

Factor typeExamples
Abiotic (non-living)Light intensity, temperature, water/moisture, pH, mineral ions in soil, oxygen/CO₂ levels, wind speed.
Biotic (living)Food availability, predators, pathogens/parasites, competition, mutualism.

2. Sampling techniques

Quadrats

  1. Use a fixed quadrat (e.g. 0.5 m × 0.5 m) placed randomly to count organisms or estimate cover.
  2. Random sampling: use a random number generator for coordinates so the sample is unbiased.
  3. Calculate mean count per quadrat, then scale up to whole habitat: total population = mean × (habitat area ÷ quadrat area).

Transects

A line laid across an area; quadrats placed at intervals along it. Used to investigate how distribution changes along a gradient (e.g. light from a tree to open ground).

Capture-mark-recapture

Used for mobile animals.

  1. Capture sample C₁; mark and release.
  2. Wait for them to mix back into the population.
  3. Capture another sample C₂; count how many are marked (M).
  4. Estimate population size N = (C₁ × C₂) ÷ M.
Mark-recapture assumes: marks don't harm or change behaviour; marked animals mix randomly; no births/deaths/migration between samples. State at least one assumption when asked about reliability.

3. Distribution and biodiversity

Biodiversity is the variety of all living organisms in an ecosystem. Higher biodiversity → more stable ecosystem because more species can fill each role.

4. Human effects on populations

Human activityEffect
DeforestationLoss of habitat → reduced biodiversity; reduces CO₂ absorption.
Burning fossil fuels↑ CO₂ → climate change; air pollution → respiratory disease in humans.
Sulfur dioxide emissionsAcid rain → leaches nutrients from soil; damages forests; lowers lake pH.
Eutrophication (fertiliser runoff)Algal bloom → blocks light → plants die → bacteria respire on them → O₂ depleted → fish die.
SewageIncreases bacteria → BOD ↑ → O₂ depleted.
PlasticsMarine animals ingest microplastics; entangle in fishing gear.
Overfishing / overhuntingPopulation crash; can drive species to extinction.
