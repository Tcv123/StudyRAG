3. Extra plant cell organelles

  • Cell wall (cellulose) — strengthens cell, prevents bursting.
  • Chloroplasts — site of photosynthesis (contain chlorophyll).
  • Permanent vacuole — sap maintains turgor pressure.

4. Microscopy — how we know

MicroscopeLight sourceMax magnificationMax resolutionLiving?
LightVisible light×1500~200 nmYes
ElectronBeam of electrons×2,000,000~0.2 nmNo (vacuum)
Magnification = size of image ÷ real size.
Resolution = smallest distance between two points that can still be seen as separate.

Worked example

An image of a mitochondrion is 30 mm long. The real organelle is 3 μm. Calculate the magnification.

  1. 30 mm = 30 000 μm.
  2. Magnification = 30 000 ÷ 3 = ×10 000.

5. Mitochondria and metabolic rate

Cells with high energy demand (sperm cells, muscle cells, liver cells) contain many mitochondria. Each mitochondrion has a folded inner membrane (cristae) which gives a large surface area for aerobic respiration enzymes.

6. Specialised cells

CellAdaptation
SpermTail (movement); many mitochondria; acrosome with enzymes.
Egg / ovumLarge food store; membrane changes after fertilisation to block other sperm.

Ciliated epithelialCilia move mucus along airways.

Xylem vesselHollow dead cells; lignin walls for support.
Phloem sieve tubeSieve plates between cells; companion cells supply ATP.
If asked to "explain how a cell is adapted", always link the adaptation to its function with "this allows…" or "so that…". E.g. "Many mitochondria → release lots of ATP → so the cell can carry out active transport / contract".
