B3.1 What happens during photosynthesis?

OCR B GCSE Biology (J257) Twenty First Century Science — Topic B3: Living together — food and ecosystems

1. The photosynthesis equation

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants and algae use light energy to convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen. It takes place in the chloroplasts, which contain the green pigment chlorophyll.
Word: carbon dioxide + water →(light) glucose + oxygen
Symbol: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Photosynthesis is an endothermic reaction — it absorbs energy from light. The energy is stored in the chemical bonds of glucose.

3. Limiting factors

The rate of photosynthesis depends on three main factors. The factor in shortest supply at any moment is the limiting factor.

FactorEffect
Light intensityRate ↑ as light ↑ until another factor becomes limiting.
Carbon dioxide concentrationRate ↑ as CO₂ ↑ until another factor limits (often around 0.4%).
TemperatureRate ↑ with temperature up to optimum (~25-30 °C); enzymes denature above ~40 °C.
Chlorophyll concentrationAffected by mineral deficiency or disease (e.g. magnesium for chlorophyll).
A graph that levels off shows another factor is limiting. To increase rate further, the new limiting factor must be raised. In commercial greenhouses, growers control all three factors (lights, heaters, CO₂ generators) — costs vs increased yield.

4. Inverse-square law (light)

Light intensity ∝ 1 / d²

Halving the distance from a lamp quadruples the light intensity. In photosynthesis practicals (PAG 5) plot rate against 1/d² rather than against d.
