B5.2 How does the nervous system help us respond to changes?

OCR B GCSE Biology (J257) Twenty First Century Science — Topic B5: The human body — staying alive

1. Stimulus → response

A stimulus is a change in the environment. It is detected by receptors; information passes via the nervous system to effectors (muscles or glands), which carry out a response.

2. The nervous system structure

PartRole
CNS — central nervous systemBrain + spinal cord; coordinates all actions.
PNS — peripheral nervous systemSensory and motor neurones connecting CNS to receptors and effectors.

3. Neurones

TypeRole
Sensory neuroneCarries impulses from receptor to CNS.
Relay neuroneConnects sensory to motor inside the CNS.
Motor neuroneCarries impulses from CNS to effector.

Neurones have a long axon (carries signal far), branched dendrites (many connections), and a myelin sheath (insulates and speeds up impulses).

4. Synapses

Where two neurones meet there is a tiny gap — the synapse. The signal crosses by:

  1. Impulse arrives at axon terminal.
  2. Vesicles release neurotransmitter chemicals into the gap.
  3. Neurotransmitter diffuses across, binds to receptors on the next neurone, and starts a new impulse.

5. The reflex arc

A reflex action is a fast, automatic response that bypasses conscious thought, protecting the body. The pathway is: stimulus → receptor → sensory neurone → relay neurone (in spinal cord) → motor neurone → effector → response.

Example — withdrawing hand from a hot object

  1. Heat receptor in skin detects high temperature.
  2. Sensory neurone carries impulse to spinal cord.
  3. Relay neurone in spinal cord transfers impulse.
  4. Motor neurone carries impulse to bicep muscle.
  5. Bicep contracts → hand pulls away.
Reflex arcs are FAST because they don't go to the brain for processing. State this in 6-mark essays on reflexes.

6. The brain

RegionFunction
Cerebral cortexConscious thought, memory, language, intelligence.
CerebellumBalance, coordination of movement.
MedullaUnconscious activities — heartbeat, breathing.
HypothalamusRegulates temperature and water balance.

Studying brain function

  • Patients with brain damage — observe lost abilities.
  • Electrical stimulation during surgery — see effects on body.
  • MRI / fMRI scans — image active brain regions during tasks.

7. The eye

PartFunction
CorneaTransparent; refracts light onto retina.
IrisMuscles control pupil size, regulating light entering.
LensRefracts light to focus image on retina.
Ciliary muscles & suspensory ligamentsChange lens shape to focus on near or distant objects (accommodation).
RetinaLight receptors (rods + cones); cones detect colour.
Optic nerveCarries impulses to the brain.

Common eye problems

  • Long-sighted (hyperopia) — eyeball too short / lens too thin → image focuses behind retina. Corrected with convex lens.
  • Short-sighted (myopia) — eyeball too long / lens too thick → image focuses in front of retina. Corrected with concave lens.
  • Other treatments: contact lenses, laser eye surgery, replacement lens implants.
