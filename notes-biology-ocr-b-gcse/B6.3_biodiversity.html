B6.3 How is biodiversity affected by human activity?

OCR B GCSE Biology (J257) Twenty First Century Science — Topic B6: Life on Earth — past, present and future

1. Biodiversity and stability

Biodiversity is the variety of all living organisms within an ecosystem, on a continent or on the whole Earth. It includes the variety of species, their genetic variation, and the variety of ecosystems. Higher biodiversity makes ecosystems more stable.

2. Human impacts on biodiversity

ActivityEffect on biodiversity
Population growth and resource useMore demand for food, fuel, materials and waste disposal.
DeforestationHabitat loss; reduces species variety; reduces CO₂ uptake by plants.
Burning fossil fuels↑ atmospheric CO₂ → climate change → habitat shifts → species displacement.
Air pollution (SO₂, NOx)Acid rain damages forests, lowers lake pH, kills sensitive species.
Water pollution (sewage, fertilisers)Eutrophication: algal bloom → O₂ depletion → fish die.
Land pollution (toxic waste, plastics)Bioaccumulation through food chains; entanglement of marine life.
Overfishing / overhuntingPopulation crashes; can drive species extinct (cod, blue whales historically).
Introduced speciesOutcompete natives, spread disease (grey squirrels, Japanese knotweed).

3. Climate change

Greenhouse gases — CO₂, methane (CH₄), water vapour — trap infrared radiation from Earth's surface, raising average global temperature. Consequences include:

  • Melting polar ice and rising sea levels.
  • More extreme weather events.
  • Species migration as habitat ranges shift; some species can't adapt and die out.
  • Disruption to seasonal cycles (e.g. earlier spring, mismatch between flowering and pollinators).

4. Conservation

MethodHow it works
Protected areasNational Parks, SSSIs, marine reserves protect habitats.
Captive breedingBreed endangered species in zoos; release into wild.
Seed banks / gene banksPreserve genetic material of plants and animals.
Reintroduction programmese.g. red kites, beavers in the UK.
International agreementsCITES restricts trade in endangered species.
Education and policyReduce demand for products that drive habitat loss (e.g. unsustainable palm oil).
