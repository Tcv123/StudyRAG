1. Diagnosis methods

MethodUse
Symptoms + medical historyFirst step — fever, rash, cough patterns suggest different pathogens.
Microscopy of samplesIdentify protists (e.g. Plasmodium in blood films) or fungal hyphae.
Culture on agarBacteria grown from samples; colony shape & Gram-stain identify species.
Antibody / antigen tests (immunoassay)Detect viral antigens (e.g. lateral-flow tests for COVID-19) or antibodies after infection.
PCR / DNA sequencingDetect pathogen DNA/RNA with high specificity (HIV, COVID-19 PCR test).

2. Monoclonal antibodies (mAbs)

Monoclonal antibodies are identical antibodies produced by clones of one B-lymphocyte. Each is specific to one antigen, so they can target a single substance with high precision.

How they are made

  1. A mouse is injected with the antigen so it makes antibody-producing B-lymphocytes.
  2. Lymphocytes are removed and fused with a tumour cell to make a hybridoma (divides indefinitely AND produces antibodies).
  3. Single hybridomas are cloned and screened.
  4. The chosen clone is grown in fermenters and antibodies are purified.

Uses

ApplicationHow it works
Pregnancy testsmAbs bind to hCG hormone in urine; coloured line shows result.
Cancer detection / treatmentmAbs bind antigens on tumour cells; deliver drugs or radioactive markers directly to the tumour.
Lab tests / researchIdentify and quantify proteins (ELISAs).
TherapyTreat rheumatoid arthritis, infections (mAbs against COVID-19 spike protein).
Many mAb questions ask "advantages and disadvantages". Advantages: highly specific so target the disease without harming healthy cells. Disadvantages: side-effects worse than expected; ethical concerns over animal use; expensive to produce.

3. Drug development

Once a pathogen has been identified, scientists screen molecules that disrupt its life cycle. Trials follow the steps in B2.3 (preclinical → phase 1/2/3 → approval). Drugs include:

  • Antibiotics — bacteria only.
  • Antivirals — slow viral replication (acyclovir for herpes, oseltamivir for flu).
  • Antifungals — clotrimazole.
  • Antimalarials — chloroquine, artemisinin combinations.
