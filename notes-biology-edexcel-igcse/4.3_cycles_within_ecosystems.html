4.3 Cycles within Ecosystems

Edexcel International GCSE Biology (4BI1) — Paper 2

4.3.1 The water cycle

  • Evaporation from oceans, rivers, lakes, soil.
  • Transpiration from plant leaves (stomata).
  • Condensation in the atmosphere → clouds.
  • Precipitation (rain, snow, hail) → returns to land and sea.
  • Water flows overland (runoff) or infiltrates soil (groundwater).

4.3.2 The carbon cycle

Key processes

  • Photosynthesis removes CO₂ from atmosphere → organic carbon.
  • Respiration (by all organisms) returns CO₂.
  • Decomposition returns CO₂ when decomposers respire.
  • Combustion of fossil fuels and biomass releases CO₂.

4.3.3 The nitrogen cycle

Key roles of bacteria:

  • Nitrogen-fixing bacteria (e.g. Rhizobium in root nodules of legumes) convert N₂ gas → ammonia.
  • Decomposers (putrefying bacteria) break down proteins from dead organisms → ammonia.
  • Nitrifying bacteria (e.g. Nitrosomonas, Nitrobacter) oxidise ammonia → nitrites → nitrates.
  • Denitrifying bacteria (anaerobic, in waterlogged soil) convert nitrates → N₂ gas, returning it to the atmosphere.

Plants absorb nitrate from soil → build amino acids → proteins. Animals get nitrogen by eating plants or other animals.

Waterlogged soil is infertile because anaerobic conditions favour denitrifying bacteria, which remove soluble nitrates. Legume rotation adds nitrates naturally via root-nodule fixation.
