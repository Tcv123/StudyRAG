4.4 Human Influences on the Environment

Edexcel International GCSE Biology (4BI1) — Paper 2

4.4.1 Greenhouse effect and climate change

Greenhouse gases (mainly CO₂ and CH₄) trap infrared radiation that would otherwise escape to space. Human activity — burning fossil fuels, deforestation, intensive livestock, landfill — has increased atmospheric concentrations, causing enhanced greenhouse effect and global warming.

Consequences:

  • Sea level rise from ice melt and thermal expansion
  • More frequent extreme weather events
  • Shifts in species ranges; pollinator–plant mismatches
  • Coral bleaching
  • Ocean acidification as CO₂ dissolves forming carbonic acid

4.4.2 Eutrophication

Excess nutrients (nitrates, phosphates) from fertilisers or sewage wash into lakes and rivers:

  1. Algal bloom on the surface.
  2. Light blocked → submerged plants die.
  3. Decomposers multiply, using dissolved O₂.
  4. Fish and other aerobic organisms suffocate.

4.4.3 Acid rain

SO₂ from burning sulfur-containing fossil fuels and NOₓ from vehicle exhausts react with water vapour → H₂SO₃ / H₂SO₄ / HNO₃ → acid rain (pH < 5). Damages trees, acidifies lakes, corrodes stonework.

4.4.4 Ozone layer and CFCs

CFCs released from old fridges, aerosols and air-con units destroy stratospheric ozone. Thinner ozone layer → more UV-B reaches surface → skin cancer, cataracts, damage to plankton. Montreal Protocol (1987) banned CFCs; the ozone layer is recovering.

4.4.5 Deforestation and biodiversity loss

  • Loss of habitats and species extinction
  • Soil erosion, flooding, reduced water cycling
  • Release of stored carbon (CO₂) as trees burn or decay
  • Loss of potential medical and crop diversity

4.4.6 Sustainable practices

  • Reforestation & afforestation
  • Renewable energy (solar, wind)
  • Fishing quotas, larger mesh sizes, no-take zones
  • Protected areas (CITES, national parks)
  • Seed banks (e.g. Svalbard)
  • Reduce, reuse, recycle
When discussing climate solutions, always weigh benefits AND costs. Wind power: no CO₂ but intermittent and visual impact. Nuclear: no CO₂ but radioactive waste. There is rarely one "best" solution.
