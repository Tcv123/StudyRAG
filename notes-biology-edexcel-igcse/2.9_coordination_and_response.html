2.9 Coordination & Response

Edexcel International GCSE Biology (4BI1) — Paper 1

2.9.1 Plant responses — tropisms

  • Phototropism: shoots grow towards light (positive); roots away (negative).
  • Geotropism (gravitropism): roots grow downwards (positive); shoots upwards (negative).

Auxin (made at tips) promotes cell elongation in shoots but inhibits it in roots. In unilateral light, auxin accumulates on the shaded side of a shoot → cells there elongate more → shoot bends towards the light.

2.9.2 The nervous system

CNS (brain + spinal cord) + PNS (all other nerves). Uses electrical impulses in neurones — fast and localised.

Synapse

  • Gap between neurones.
  • Electrical impulse arrives → vesicles release neurotransmitter → diffuses across synapse → binds receptors on next neurone → new impulse starts.
  • One-way transmission only (vesicles only on pre-synaptic side).

2.9.3 The eye

  • Cornea & lens refract light onto the retina (contains rods + cones).
  • Iris controls pupil size — circular muscles contract in bright light (pupil constricts); radial muscles contract in dim light (pupil dilates).
  • Accommodation — near object: ciliary muscles contract, suspensory ligaments slacken, lens becomes fatter. Distant object: muscles relax, ligaments tight, lens flatter.

2.9.4 Hormones and homeostasis

Hormones are chemical messengers secreted by endocrine glands into the blood. Slower than nerves but longer-lasting.

HormoneFromEffect
InsulinPancreas (β-cells)Lowers blood glucose (→ glycogen)
GlucagonPancreas (α-cells)Raises blood glucose (← glycogen)
AdrenalineAdrenal glands"Fight or flight" — ↑ heart rate, ↑ glucose
ADHPituitaryMore water reabsorption at kidney
FSH, LH, oestrogen, progesteronePituitary / ovariesMenstrual cycle
TestosteroneTestesMale secondary sexual characteristics

Blood glucose control (negative feedback)

↑ glucose → insulin → liver takes up glucose as glycogen → glucose falls → insulin falls.
↓ glucose → glucagon → liver breaks down glycogen → glucose rises → glucagon falls.

Type 1 diabetes: pancreas stops making insulin — treated with injections. Type 2 diabetes: body cells become insulin-resistant — managed with diet, exercise, medication.

Thermoregulation

  • Too hot: vasodilation, sweating, hair flat.
  • Too cold: vasoconstriction, shivering, hair erect (goosebumps), reduced sweating.
