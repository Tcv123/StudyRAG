4.1 The Organism in its Environment

Edexcel International GCSE Biology (4BI1) — Paper 2

4.1.1 Key terms

Population — all organisms of one species in an area at one time.
Community — all populations of different species in the same area.
Ecosystem — a community + its abiotic environment.
Habitat — the place where an organism lives.
Niche — the role of an organism in its ecosystem.

4.1.3 Sampling methods

  • Quadrat — frame (e.g. 0.5 m × 0.5 m) placed randomly; count individuals or estimate % cover. Good for plants / slow-moving organisms.
  • Transect — line across a habitat (e.g. sea to land). Place quadrats at regular intervals to study how distribution changes with an environmental gradient.
  • Pooter — suck small insects from leaf litter into a chamber for identification.
  • Mark-release-recapture — for mobile animals. Mark, release, recapture later. Estimated population N = (M × C) / R (Lincoln index), where M = marked in first sample, C = total in second sample, R = recaptured marked individuals.
Key assumptions for MRR: (1) population closed (no births/deaths/migration during study), (2) mark doesn't affect survival or behaviour, (3) marked individuals mix randomly with the rest. Any violation gives a biased estimate.

4.1.4 Worked example (MRR)

You mark 60 woodlice and release them. A few days later you capture 50, of which 12 are marked.
N = (60 × 50) ÷ 12 = 250 woodlice.

4.1.5 Why random sampling?

Non-random sampling introduces bias (e.g. sampling visible areas first). Random coordinates avoid this and give a more representative estimate.
