3.1.3 Human reproduction

  • Sperm (from testes): small, tail, many mitochondria, haploid nucleus, acrosome with enzymes.
  • Ovum (from ovaries): large, food reserves (cytoplasm), jelly coat, haploid nucleus.
  • Fertilisation in oviduct → zygote → embryo → implants in uterus lining.
  • Placenta: exchange of O₂, glucose, amino acids to fetus; CO₂, urea away. Does not mix mother's and fetus's blood.
  • Amnion encloses amniotic fluid — cushions fetus.

Menstrual cycle (28 days)

  • Days 1–5: menstruation (lining shed).
  • Days 6–13: FSH → follicle grows; oestrogen rebuilds lining.
  • Day 14: LH surge → ovulation.
  • Days 15–28: corpus luteum → progesterone maintains lining. If no fertilisation, progesterone falls, lining shed → cycle restarts.

3.1.4 STIs and HIV

HIV attacks helper T-lymphocytes — without treatment leads to AIDS. Transmitted via body fluids (unprotected sex, shared needles, mother-to-baby). Reduce spread: condoms, screening donated blood, antiretroviral therapy.

The placenta is a selective barrier — not a complete one. Alcohol, nicotine, heroin and some viruses (e.g. rubella) can cross; hence the advice to avoid these in pregnancy.
