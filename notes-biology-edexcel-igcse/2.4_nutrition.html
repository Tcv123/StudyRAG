2.4.1 Plant Nutrition — Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis converts light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose.
6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ (light, chlorophyll).

Limiting factors

  • Light intensity — provides energy. Rate increases with light then plateaus.
  • CO₂ concentration — a reactant. In UK air, CO₂ is typically the limiting factor on a sunny day.
  • Temperature — affects enzyme-controlled reactions. Optimal around 25–30 °C; denatured above 45 °C.
Commercial growers enrich CO₂, heat glasshouses and use artificial lights at night to keep all three above limiting values.

The alimentary canal and digestion

Mouth (amylase → starch to maltose) → oesophagus → stomach (pepsin + HCl → proteins) → small intestine (pancreatic amylase, trypsin, lipase; bile emulsifies fat; villi absorb) → large intestine (water reabsorbed) → rectum & anus (egestion).

Absorption at the villi

Villi are finger-like projections in the small intestine. Each has: large surface area (microvilli), thin wall (1 cell), rich capillary network, central lacteal (for lipids), and many mitochondria for active transport of glucose and amino acids.

Bile is NOT an enzyme. It is made by the liver, stored in the gall bladder, and emulsifies fats into smaller droplets — increasing surface area for lipase. It also neutralises stomach acid to provide optimum pH for intestinal enzymes.
