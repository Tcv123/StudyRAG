2.7.2 Transpiration

Water evaporates from mesophyll cells and diffuses out through stomata. Water is replaced via xylem (transpiration pull + cohesion).

Factors that increase rate: high temperature, low humidity, wind, bright light. Investigate using a potometer — measures water uptake (≈ transpiration rate).

2.7.4 The heart and circulation

Route through the heart (from body)

Vena cava → right atrium → right ventricle → pulmonary artery → lungs → pulmonary vein → left atrium → left ventricle → aorta → body.

Left ventricle wall is thicker than the right because it must pump blood at higher pressure to the whole body; the right only pumps to the nearby lungs.
