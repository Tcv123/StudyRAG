2.8 Excretion

Edexcel International GCSE Biology (4BI1) — Paper 1

2.8.1 What is excretion?

The removal of metabolic waste products (and substances in excess) from the body. Major wastes: CO₂ (respiration) excreted by lungs; urea (from breakdown of excess amino acids in liver) excreted by kidneys; excess water and salts excreted by kidneys and sweat glands.

Plants excrete O₂ and CO₂ through stomata and lenticels; excess water as vapour (transpiration). Some waste ions and organic molecules may be stored in leaves that fall (leaf senescence).

2.8.2 The urinary system

Kidneys (×2) → ureters → bladder → urethra.
Each kidney contains ~1 million nephrons where urine is made.

Three key processes

  1. Ultrafiltration at glomerulus: high blood pressure forces small molecules (water, glucose, urea, ions) into Bowman's capsule. Large molecules (proteins) and blood cells stay in blood.
  2. Selective reabsorption in proximal tubule: all glucose, most salts, and much water returned to blood by active transport and osmosis.
  3. Water reabsorption (ADH-controlled) in collecting duct: ADH from pituitary makes walls more permeable when blood too concentrated. Small volume of concentrated urine excreted.

2.8.3 ADH — negative feedback

  • Blood too concentrated (e.g. sweating, salty food): osmoreceptors → more ADH → more H₂O reabsorbed → small dark urine.
  • Blood too dilute (drunk lots of water): less ADH → less H₂O reabsorbed → large pale urine.
