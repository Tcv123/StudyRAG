For the triangle and parallelogram, $h$ must be the perpendicular height — the vertical distance between the parallel sides, not the slant side.
Find the area of a trapezium with parallel sides 8 cm and 14 cm and perpendicular height 5 cm.

$A = \tfrac{1}{2}(8+14) \times 5 = \tfrac{1}{2} \times 22 \times 5 = \mathbf{55\,\text{cm}^2}$
A triangle has base 9 cm and area 27 cm². Find the perpendicular height.

$27 = \tfrac{1}{2} \times 9 \times h \Rightarrow h = 27 \times 2 \div 9 = \mathbf{6\,\text{cm}}$

4.2.2 Area and Circumference of Circles

$$\text{Circumference: } C = 2\pi r = \pi d$$ $$\text{Area: } A = \pi r^2$$

For an arc of angle $\theta°$: $\;\text{Arc length} = \dfrac{\theta}{360} \times 2\pi r$

For a sector of angle $\theta°$: $\;\text{Sector area} = \dfrac{\theta}{360} \times \pi r^2$

Find the circumference and area of a circle with radius 7 cm. (Leave answers in terms of $\pi$.)

$C = 2\pi \times 7 = 14\pi \approx \mathbf{44.0\,\text{cm}}$
$A = \pi \times 7^2 = 49\pi \approx \mathbf{153.9\,\text{cm}^2}$
A sector has radius 12 cm and angle $150°$. Find its arc length and area.

Arc length = $\dfrac{150}{360} \times 2\pi \times 12 = \dfrac{5}{12} \times 24\pi = 10\pi \approx \mathbf{31.4\,\text{cm}}$

Sector area = $\dfrac{150}{360} \times \pi \times 12^2 = \dfrac{5}{12} \times 144\pi = 60\pi \approx \mathbf{188.5\,\text{cm}^2}$
If the question says "leave in terms of $\pi$", do not press the $\pi$ button on your calculator — just write $49\pi$ etc. If it asks for a decimal, use $\pi \approx 3.14159\ldots$

4.2.3 Area of Composite Shapes

A composite shape is made up of two or more standard shapes joined together. Split it into rectangles, triangles, circles, etc. and add or subtract areas.
Find the area of an L-shaped figure formed by a rectangle 10 cm × 8 cm with a 4 cm × 3 cm rectangle cut from one corner.

Area of full rectangle: $10 \times 8 = 80\,\text{cm}^2$
Area removed: $4 \times 3 = 12\,\text{cm}^2$
Composite area = $80 - 12 = \mathbf{68\,\text{cm}^2}$
A shape consists of a rectangle 6 cm × 4 cm with a semicircle of diameter 4 cm attached to one end. Find the total area.

Rectangle area = $6 \times 4 = 24\,\text{cm}^2$
Semicircle: radius = 2 cm; area = $\tfrac{1}{2}\pi \times 2^2 = 2\pi \approx 6.28\,\text{cm}^2$
Total = $24 + 2\pi \approx \mathbf{30.3\,\text{cm}^2}$

Find the volume and total surface area of a cylinder with radius 5 cm and height 12 cm.

A triangular prism has a right-angled triangular cross-section with legs 3 cm and 4 cm (hypotenuse 5 cm). The prism has length 10 cm. Find its volume and surface area.

Volume: Cross-section area = $\tfrac{1}{2} \times 3 \times 4 = 6\,\text{cm}^2$
$V = 6 \times 10 = \mathbf{60\,\text{cm}^3}$

Surface area:
2 triangular ends: $2 \times 6 = 12\,\text{cm}^2$
3 rectangular faces: $(3 \times 10) + (4 \times 10) + (5 \times 10) = 30 + 40 + 50 = 120\,\text{cm}^2$
Total SA = $12 + 120 = \mathbf{132\,\text{cm}^2}$

Find the volume of a cone with radius 6 cm and height 8 cm.

$V = \dfrac{1}{3} \times \pi \times 6^2 \times 8 = \dfrac{1}{3} \times 36\pi \times 8 = 96\pi \approx \mathbf{301.6\,\text{cm}^3}$
Find the total surface area of a cone with radius 5 cm and height 12 cm.

Slant height: $l = \sqrt{5^2 + 12^2} = \sqrt{25+144} = \sqrt{169} = 13\,\text{cm}$
Curved SA = $\pi \times 5 \times 13 = 65\pi$
Base area = $\pi \times 5^2 = 25\pi$
Total SA = $65\pi + 25\pi = 90\pi \approx \mathbf{282.7\,\text{cm}^2}$
A sphere has volume $288\pi\,\text{cm}^3$. Find its radius.

$\dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3 = 288\pi$
$r^3 = 288 \times \dfrac{3}{4} = 216$
$r = \sqrt[3]{216} = \mathbf{6\,\text{cm}}$
A square-based pyramid has base side 9 cm and perpendicular height 10 cm. Find its volume.

Base area $A = 9^2 = 81\,\text{cm}^2$
$V = \dfrac{1}{3} \times 81 \times 10 = \mathbf{270\,\text{cm}^3}$

4.2.6 Frustums Higher

A frustum is the part of a cone left after the top (a smaller, similar cone) is removed by a cut parallel to the base.
Volume of frustum = Volume of large cone $-$ Volume of small cone: $$V_{\text{frustum}} = \frac{1}{3}\pi R^2 H - \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h = \frac{\pi}{3}(R^2 H - r^2 h)$$ where $R$ = radius of base, $r$ = radius of top, $H$ = height of full cone, $h$ = height of removed small cone.
To find $H$ and $h$, use the similar triangle ratio $\dfrac{r}{R} = \dfrac{h}{H}$. If you are given only the height of the frustum ($H - h$), you will need to set up a ratio to find $H$ and $h$ separately.
A frustum is formed by removing a small cone of height 6 cm and radius 2 cm from a full cone of height 18 cm and radius 6 cm. Find the volume of the frustum.

Check similarity ratio: $r/R = 2/6 = 1/3$ and $h/H = 6/18 = 1/3$ ✓ (consistent)

$V_{\text{large}} = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi \times 6^2 \times 18 = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi \times 36 \times 18 = 216\pi$
$V_{\text{small}} = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi \times 2^2 \times 6 = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi \times 4 \times 6 = 8\pi$
$V_{\text{frustum}} = 216\pi - 8\pi = 208\pi \approx \mathbf{653.5\,\text{cm}^3}$

4.2.7 Units of Area and Volume

Units of length: $1\,\text{km} = 1000\,\text{m}$; $1\,\text{m} = 100\,\text{cm}$; $1\,\text{cm} = 10\,\text{mm}$

Units of area: $$1\,\text{m}^2 = 10{,}000\,\text{cm}^2 = 100^2\,\text{cm}^2$$ $$1\,\text{km}^2 = 1{,}000{,}000\,\text{m}^2$$ $$1\,\text{hectare (ha)} = 10{,}000\,\text{m}^2$$ Units of volume: $$1\,\text{m}^3 = 1{,}000{,}000\,\text{cm}^3 = 100^3\,\text{cm}^3$$ $$1\,\text{litre} = 1000\,\text{cm}^3 = 1000\,\text{ml}$$ $$1\,\text{m}^3 = 1000\,\text{litres}$$

Remember: when converting units of area, square the linear conversion factor. When converting units of volume, cube the linear conversion factor. E.g., $1\,\text{m} = 100\,\text{cm}$, so $1\,\text{m}^2 = 100^2 = 10{,}000\,\text{cm}^2$ and $1\,\text{m}^3 = 100^3 = 1{,}000{,}000\,\text{cm}^3$.
A tank has volume $0.36\,\text{m}^3$. How many litres of water does it hold?

$0.36\,\text{m}^3 = 0.36 \times 1{,}000{,}000\,\text{cm}^3 = 360{,}000\,\text{cm}^3$
Litres = $360{,}000 \div 1000 = \mathbf{360\,\text{litres}}$
