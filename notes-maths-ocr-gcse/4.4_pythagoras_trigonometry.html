OCR GCSE Mathematics J560 — Component 4 (Geometry and Measures) — Papers 1, 2, 3

4.4.1 Pythagoras' Theorem

In any right-angled triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides. $$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$ where $c$ is the hypotenuse.
Finding the hypotenuse:
$c = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$

Finding a shorter side:
$a = \sqrt{c^2 - b^2}$
A right-angled triangle has legs 5 cm and 12 cm. Find the hypotenuse.

$c = \sqrt{5^2 + 12^2} = \sqrt{25 + 144} = \sqrt{169} = \mathbf{13}$ cm
The hypotenuse is 17 cm and one leg is 8 cm. Find the other leg.

$a = \sqrt{17^2 - 8^2} = \sqrt{289 - 64} = \sqrt{225} = \mathbf{15}$ cm

Pythagoras in 3D

For a cuboid with dimensions $l \times w \times h$, the space diagonal $d$ is:

$$d = \sqrt{l^2 + w^2 + h^2}$$
A cuboid is 6 cm long, 4 cm wide, 3 cm tall. Find the length of the space diagonal.

First find the base diagonal: $\sqrt{6^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{52}$
Then: $d = \sqrt{(\sqrt{52})^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{52 + 9} = \sqrt{61} \approx \mathbf{7.81}$ cm
Always identify the hypotenuse first — it is opposite the right angle and is the longest side. If the answer is not a whole number, leave it as a surd ($\sqrt{}$) unless asked to round.

4.4.2 SOHCAHTOA (Right-Angled Trigonometry)

For a right-angled triangle, the three basic trigonometric ratios relate an angle $\theta$ to the sides:
  • Opposite — the side opposite to $\theta$
  • Adjacent — the side next to $\theta$ (not the hypotenuse)
  • Hypotenuse — the longest side (opposite the right angle)
SOH
$\sin\theta = \dfrac{\text{Opp}}{\text{Hyp}}$
CAH
$\cos\theta = \dfrac{\text{Adj}}{\text{Hyp}}$
TOA
$\tan\theta = \dfrac{\text{Opp}}{\text{Adj}}$
Find a missing side: In triangle ABC, angle B = 35°, hypotenuse AB = 12 cm. Find BC (adjacent).

Using CAH: $\cos 35° = \dfrac{BC}{12}$
$BC = 12 \times \cos 35° = 12 \times 0.8192 \approx \mathbf{9.83}$ cm
Find a missing angle: Opposite = 7 cm, hypotenuse = 10 cm. Find $\theta$.

Using SOH: $\sin\theta = \dfrac{7}{10} = 0.7$
$\theta = \sin^{-1}(0.7) \approx \mathbf{44.4°}$

Symmetry and Solutions

  • $\sin(180° - x) = \sin x$ — sine is symmetric about 90°
  • $\cos(360° - x) = \cos x$ — cosine is symmetric about 0°/360°
  • $\cos(-x) = \cos x$ — cosine is an even function
  • If $\sin\theta = k$, the second solution in $[0°, 360°]$ is $180° - \theta$
  • If $\cos\theta = k$, the second solution in $[0°, 360°]$ is $360° - \theta$
Solve $\sin\theta = 0.6$ for $0° \le \theta \le 360°$.

$\theta = \sin^{-1}(0.6) = 36.87°$
Second solution: $180° - 36.87° = 143.13°$
$\theta \approx 36.9°$ or $143.1°$

4.4.4 Sine Rule H

For any triangle with sides $a, b, c$ opposite to angles $A, B, C$ respectively: $$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$$ Rearranged for finding angles: $$\frac{\sin A}{a} = \frac{\sin B}{b} = \frac{\sin C}{c}$$

Use the Sine Rule when you know: two angles and one side (AAS / ASA), or two sides and a non-included angle (SSA).

In triangle ABC: $a = 8$ cm, $A = 40°$, $B = 70°$. Find $b$.

$\dfrac{b}{\sin 70°} = \dfrac{8}{\sin 40°}$

$b = \dfrac{8 \times \sin 70°}{\sin 40°} = \dfrac{8 \times 0.9397}{0.6428} \approx \mathbf{11.7}$ cm
Find angle $B$ given $b = 12$ cm, $a = 9$ cm, $A = 55°$.

$\dfrac{\sin B}{12} = \dfrac{\sin 55°}{9}$

$\sin B = \dfrac{12 \times \sin 55°}{9} = \dfrac{12 \times 0.8192}{9} = 1.092...$

Since $\sin B > 1$, no solution exists — the triangle is impossible with these measurements.

The Ambiguous Case (SSA)

When given two sides and a non-included angle, there may be 0, 1, or 2 valid triangles. If $\sin B < 1$, check whether $B$ (acute) and $180° - B$ (obtuse) both give a valid triangle (i.e. sum of angles < 180°).

For angles, use the form $\frac{\sin A}{a}$ (sine over side). For sides, use $\frac{a}{\sin A}$ (side over sine). Only use two parts of the rule at once — pick the pair with the most knowns.

4.4.5 Cosine Rule H

For any triangle: $$a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$$ Rearranged to find an angle: $$\cos A = \frac{b^2 + c^2 - a^2}{2bc}$$

Use the Cosine Rule when you know: two sides and the included angle (SAS), or all three sides (SSS).

Sides $b = 7$, $c = 10$, included angle $A = 50°$. Find $a$.

$a^2 = 7^2 + 10^2 - 2(7)(10)\cos 50°$
$a^2 = 49 + 100 - 140 \times 0.6428 = 149 - 89.99 = 59.01$
$a = \sqrt{59.01} \approx \mathbf{7.68}$ cm
Three sides $a = 5$, $b = 7$, $c = 9$. Find angle $A$.

$\cos A = \dfrac{7^2 + 9^2 - 5^2}{2 \times 7 \times 9} = \dfrac{49 + 81 - 25}{126} = \dfrac{105}{126} = 0.8333$

$A = \cos^{-1}(0.8333) \approx \mathbf{33.6°}$
Never use the Cosine Rule for a right-angled triangle — Pythagoras or SOHCAHTOA is simpler. The Cosine Rule is for non-right-angled triangles only.

4.4.6 Area of a Triangle H

For any triangle with two sides $a$ and $b$ and included angle $C$: $$A = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$$
Triangle with sides 8 cm and 11 cm, included angle 63°. Find the area.

$A = \frac{1}{2} \times 8 \times 11 \times \sin 63° = 44 \times 0.891 \approx \mathbf{39.2}$ cm²
The area of a triangle is 30 cm². Two sides are 10 cm and 9 cm. Find the included angle.

$30 = \frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 9 \times \sin C$
$\sin C = \dfrac{30}{45} = 0.6\overline{6}$
$C = \sin^{-1}(0.6\overline{6}) \approx \mathbf{41.8°}$
