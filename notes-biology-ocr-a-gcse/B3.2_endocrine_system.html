1. The Endocrine System

The endocrine system uses chemicals called hormones to coordinate body functions. Hormones are produced by glands and carried in the bloodstream to target organs where they have an effect. Compared with the nervous system, hormonal responses are slower but generally longer-lasting.

3. Control of Blood Glucose Concentration

Blood glucose concentration is monitored and controlled by the pancreas, which releases two hormones: insulin (when blood glucose is too high) and glucagon (when blood glucose is too low). This is an example of negative feedback — a change is detected and a response brings the system back towards normal.

4. The Menstrual Cycle

The menstrual cycle in women has a typical length of about 28 days and is controlled by four hormones: FSH, LH, oestrogen and progesterone.
HormoneFromRole
FSH (Follicle-Stimulating Hormone)PituitaryCauses an egg to mature in an ovary follicle; stimulates oestrogen release
OestrogenOvariesBuilds up the uterine lining; inhibits FSH; stimulates LH
LH (Luteinising Hormone)PituitaryTriggers ovulation (release of egg) on day 14
ProgesteroneOvaries (corpus luteum)Maintains the uterine lining; inhibits FSH and LH

6. Fertility Treatments — IVF

IVF (in vitro fertilisation) can help couples who struggle to conceive naturally:

  1. Woman is given FSH and LH to stimulate maturation of multiple eggs.
  2. Eggs are collected from the ovaries.
  3. Eggs are fertilised in a lab dish with sperm from the partner (or donor).
  4. Embryos develop for 2–5 days.
  5. One or two embryos are inserted into the womb.

Issues: emotional/physical stress; expensive; success rates ~30%; risk of multiple births; ethical concerns about leftover embryos.
