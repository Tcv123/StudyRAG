1. DNA, Genes and Chromosomes

Every cell nucleus contains chromosomes made of long molecules of DNA. A short section of DNA that codes for a particular protein is a gene. Humans have 46 chromosomes (23 pairs) in each body cell — one of each pair from the mother and one from the father.

At fertilisation, a male haploid gamete (sperm — 23 chromosomes) fuses with a female haploid gamete (egg — 23 chromosomes) to form a diploid zygote (46 chromosomes).

3. Sex Determination

Of the 23 pairs of human chromosomes, one pair are the sex chromosomes. Females are XX (two X chromosomes); males are XY. Eggs all carry an X chromosome; sperm carry either X or Y. The sex of the child is determined by whether an X-carrying or Y-carrying sperm fertilises the egg.

Punnett square for sex inheritance:

X (egg)X (egg)
X (sperm)XXXX
Y (sperm)XYXY

Result: 50% XX (female), 50% XY (male) — explains why male:female ratio at birth is approximately 1:1.

4. Monohybrid Inheritance — Punnett Squares

A monohybrid cross looks at the inheritance of a single gene with two alleles. We can predict offspring ratios using a Punnett square.

Worked example: Eye colour

Brown allele (B) is dominant over blue (b). Both parents are heterozygous (Bb).

Bb
BBBBb
bBbbb

Genotype ratio: 1 BB : 2 Bb : 1 bb
Phenotype ratio: 3 brown eyes : 1 blue eyes

Worked example: Test cross

An organism showing the dominant phenotype (brown eyes) could be BB or Bb. Crossing with a homozygous recessive (bb) reveals which:

If parent is BB:BB × bb → all offspring Bb (all brown)
If parent is Bb:Bb × bb → 50% Bb (brown), 50% bb (blue)

6. Embryo Screening and Genetic Engineering

Embryo screening (PGD)

In IVF, embryos can be screened for genetic disorders before being implanted. Pros: avoids passing on serious diseases; reduces suffering. Cons: ethical concerns about destroying embryos; "designer babies" slippery slope; expensive; not all conditions screenable.

Genetic engineering

Genetic engineering involves transferring genes between organisms. Examples:

  • Bacteria producing human insulin for diabetics — gene cut from human DNA, inserted into bacterial plasmid using restriction enzymes and ligase.
  • Golden Rice — engineered to make beta-carotene (vitamin A) to combat blindness in poor regions.
  • Pest-resistant crops (Bt corn) — bacterial toxin gene inserted into corn.

Concerns: long-term effects unknown; possible spread to wild plants; ethics of patenting living things; reduced biodiversity if monocultures dominate.

7. Variation

Source of variationExamples
GeneticEye colour, blood group, hair texture (passed down from parents via gametes)
EnvironmentalTan from sunlight; scars; weight from diet/exercise
CombinationMost traits — e.g. height influenced by genes AND nutrition

Mutation is a random change in the DNA sequence. Most mutations have no effect (silent), some are harmful, and rarely some are beneficial. Beneficial mutations are the raw material for evolution by natural selection.
